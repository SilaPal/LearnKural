The Enduring Wisdom of Thirukkural
Timeless teachings for a meaningful life

What is Thirukkural?
Thirukkural is a classic Tamil text that consists of 1,330 short couplets, or "kurals," written by the ancient poet and philosopher Thiruvalluvar. The name literally means "sacred couplets" and it's considered a masterpiece of human thought. The text is divided into three books covering:

Virtue (Aram): Deals with ethical conduct and righteous living.
Wealth (Porul): Focuses on governance, economics, and social life.
Love (Inbam): Explores the complexities of human emotions, love, and domestic life.
Each kural is a concise, seven-word couplet that encapsulates profound wisdom, making it easily memorable and impactful.

How Old is Thirukkural?
The Thirukkural is attributed to the ancient poet and philosopher **Thiruvalluvar**, who is traditionally believed to have lived around **31 BCE**. He is revered as one of the greatest literary figures in Tamil history, known for his profound insights into ethics and morality.

How it Helps for Life
Thirukkural is like a wise old friend that gives us good advice for living a happy and fair life, even today! Here's how it helps:

Be a Good Person: It teaches us to be honest, kind, and to always do the right thing. It's like a guide for being a good friend, a good family member, and a good person in general.
Be a Good Leader: It has smart ideas for leaders, like parents, teachers, or even future presidents! It teaches them how to be fair and make good decisions for everyone.
Have Happy Relationships: It shows us how to treat our family and friends with love and respect. It helps us understand how to forgive and build strong, happy connections with others.
Keep Your Mind Happy: It talks about having a "good mind" that is full of good thoughts and no bad ones. This helps us feel calm and happy inside.
For Everyone, Everywhere: The best part is that its lessons are for *everyone*, no matter where they live or what they believe. It teaches us about being good humans together!
